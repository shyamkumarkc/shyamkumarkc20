Note Kosh


Note Kosh (नोट कोष) generally refers to a "Note Reserve" or "Currency Reserve" in financial and banking contexts, especially in Nepal and South Asian countries. In the context of Nepal, Note Kosh is typically associated with the Note Department of the Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB), the central bank of Nepal. Note Kosh is a vault maintained in commercial bank by central bank in order to keep Nepalese Currency.It is maintained where there is no branch of NRB for the purpose of helping bank and fianancial instituions of particular area for easy fund or liquidity management.

The commission rate of Note Kosh is 0.40 % at the time of withdraw and 0.20 % at the time of deposit, and the transactions can be done in multiple of 1000 only. Nepal Rastra Bank has maintained note kosh in government commercial bank i.e. Rastriya Banijya Bank and Nepal Bank Limited. Among 75 note kosh, 8 note kosh are within Nepal Rastra Bank, 42 are in Rastriya Banijya Bank and 25 are in Nepal Bank Limited. Such note kosh are called "note chest" by NRB since they keep physical cash and make necessary supply which are managed by Currency Management Department and handled by designated commercial banks.

Cash deposited in note kosh can be taken at the time of calculation of Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR). It handles both newly printed notes and notes withdrawn from circulation (old, torn, or damaged

Functions

  1. Storage of Currency Notes:

    2. Acts as a secure reserve or vault where currency notes are stored in bulk.

  2. Distribution to Commercial Banks:

    3. Supplies new and fit notes to commercial banks and financial institutions across the country.

  3. Collection and Disposal of Old Notes:

    4. Collects old, damaged, or unfit notes from banks and ensures their destruction or replacement.

  4. Maintaining Currency Quality:

    5. Ensures that only clean, authentic, and usable notes are in circulation.

  5. Security and Inventory Management:

    6. Implements strict security protocols to protect national currency reserves and maintain proper inventory records.
