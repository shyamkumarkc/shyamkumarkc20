Auto Loan

Auto loan in Nepal refers to a financial product offered by banks and financial institutions in Nepal for an individual or a business to purchase vehicle(s). These loans are available for the purchase of new and used cars, and commercial vehicles.

Key Features of Auto Loans:

  1. Loan Amount:

    • The loan amount typically covers 50% to 90% of the vehicle's price, depending on the bank's policy, the applicant's creditworthiness, and the type of vehicle.
    • Borrowers are required to make a down payment for the remaining percentage of the vehicle's cost.

  2. Interest Rates:

    • Interest rates for auto loans in Nepal can vary significantly between banks and financial institutions.
    • Rates are either fixed or variable, with variable rates being tied to the Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) guidelines or the financial institution's base rate.

  3. Repayment Period:

    • Repayment tenure usually ranges from 1 to 7 years, depending on the borrower’s preference and the bank’s terms.

  4. Eligibility Criteria:

    • Applicants must demonstrate a stable income source (e.g., salaried employees, self-employed individuals, or business owners).
    • A good credit history improves the chances of approval.
    • Banks may require proof of residency and identification, such as a Citizenship Certificate or Passport.

  5. Collateral:

    • The vehicle being purchased usually serves as the collateral for the loan. In case of loan default, the bank can seize the vehicle to recover the amount.

  6. Processing Fees and Charges:

    • Banks in Nepal charge processing fees for auto loans, which range from 0.5% to 1% of the loan amount.
    • Additional charges such as valuation fees, documentation fees, or insurance premiums may apply.

    Important Considerations:

    • Borrowers should compare interest rates, processing fees, and repayment terms across institutions.
    • Loan insurance may be mandatory for the vehicle.
    • Prepayment or early repayment penalties might apply depending on the lender's policy.
