CDS & Clearing Limited (CDSC) Nepal

Introduction

CDS and Clearing Limited (CDSC) is a company incorporated under the Company Act of Nepal established by Nepal Stock Exchange Limited (NEPSE) in 2010. Its primary objective is to serve as the central depository for various financial instruments, including equities, bonds, and warrants -- by managing them in dematerialized form. CDSC handles the clearing and settlement of securities traded in NEPSE under a T+2 settlement cycle. It is responsible for the safekeeping, deposit, withdrawal, and transfer of ownership or rights of these securities.

The company is jointly owned by the Government of Nepal and Nepal Stock Exchange Limited (NEPSE). It operates under the Securities Act 2063 and the Company Act 2063 of Nepal. CDSC's vision is to be a trusted, efficient, and technology-driven central depository empowering a transparent, secure, and globally competitive capital market.

CDSC Full Form

CDSC stands for Central Depository System and Clearing Limited. It manages electronic securities settlement in Nepal’s stock market.

Core Functions of CDSC

  • Electronic settlement of stock trades
  • Demat conversion of physical shares
  • Ownership transfer processing
  • Safe custody of securities
  • ISIN generation for listed securities

Why CDSC Is Important

CDSC plays a critical role in Nepal’s capital market infrastructure by ensuring secure, transparent and efficient settlement of trades. Without CDSC, modern stock exchange operations and dematerialized share ownership would not be possible.

FAQ

What is CDSC?

It is Nepal’s official securities depository responsible for settlement and record keeping.

Is CDSC Government owned?

It is a subsidiary company promoted by NEPSE, Nepal’s national stock exchange.

Why is CDSC required?

It eliminates paper certificates and enables secure digital share ownership.
