Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB)

Introduction

Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) is the central bank of Nepal, established on April 26, 1956 (2013 Baisakh 14) under the Nepal Rastra Bank Act, 1955. It is the supreme monetary authority responsible for regulating the country’s financial system, issuing currency, maintaining price stability, and promoting sustainable economic growth.

The modern legal framework governing NRB is the Nepal Rastra Bank Act, 2058 (2002), which defines its powers, functions, and objectives to ensure financial stability and public confidence in the banking sector.

---

Main Objectives of Nepal Rastra Bank

  • Maintain price stability through effective monetary policy
  • Ensure balance of payments stability
  • Promote financial sector stability
  • Increase access to financial services
  • Develop a secure payment and settlement system
---

Core Functions of NRB

1. Currency Issuance

NRB has sole authority to issue and circulate Nepalese currency notes and coins.

2. Banker to Government

Acts as banker, advisor, and financial agent of the Government of Nepal.

3. Bankers’ Bank

Provides services to commercial banks and financial institutions.

4. Lender of Last Resort

Provides emergency liquidity support to banks facing financial crisis.

5. Clearing and Settlement Agent

Facilitates payment, clearing, and settlement systems nationwide.

---

Roles of Nepal Rastra Bank in the Economy

  • Regulates money supply and credit creation
  • Manages foreign exchange reserves
  • Supervises banks and financial institutions
  • Manages public debt
  • Maintains relations with international financial institutions
  • Formulates monetary and credit policies

    Functions, Rights and Duties Under NRB Act 2058

    1. Issue banknotes and coins
    2. Formulate and implement monetary policy
    3. Formulate foreign exchange policy
    4. Determine exchange rate system
    5. Manage foreign reserves
    6. License and regulate banks and financial institutions
    7. Act as government banker and advisor
    8. Act as banker of banks
    9. Develop payment and settlement systems
    10. Perform necessary functions to achieve objectives
    ---

    Priority Rights of NRB

    For recovery of loans, NRB holds priority rights over borrower assets including deposits and property. It may seize and sell such assets as per law.

    ---

    Privileges of Nepal Rastra Bank

    • Exemption from taxes and fees
    • No registration fee for loan documents
    • No revenue stamps required
    • No duties on import/export of currency materials
